​**🛋️ Furniture**

**1. Furniture Types (What it Does)**

*   **Sitting:**

    *   Sofas: Small (for two!), regular size, sectionals, chaise lounges, sofa beds are also an option.
    *   Chairs: Armchairs, recliners, accent chairs, dining chairs, rocking chairs, work chairs.
    *   Benches &amp; Ottomans: For your entry area, storage benches, footstools, and poufs.

*   **Tables**

    *   Dining Table: Round, rectangle, growable ones.
    *   Coffee Table: Usually short, goes `ahead` of your sofa.
    *   Side Tables / End Tables: Go `next to` your seating.
    *   Console Table: Good for entryways or `past `sofas.
    *   Study/Desk: For writing, computers, or standing.

*   **Storage**

    *   Cabinets: For showing off stuff, filing, kitchens, bathrooms.
    *   Wardrobes / Armoires: Place to put clothes.
    *   Dressers &amp; Chests: More spots for clothes, usually in your bedroom.
    *   Shelving Units: Hang on the wall, stand on their own, shelves that look like ladders.

*   **Beds**

    *   Platform Bed: Just needs a mattress.
    *   Panel Bed: Has a headboard and footboard.
    *   Bunk Bed / Loft Bed: Great for kids or small places.
    *   Storage Bed: Has drawers or lifts up for storage.

**2. What It's Made Of**

*   Wood: Real wood (lasts ages), fake wood (cheaper), wood covering (looks good).
*   Metal: Like iron, aluminum, or stainless steel – good for bed frames, tables, chairs.
*   Glass: Strong glass – for tables or cabinet doors.
*   Plastic &amp; Acrylic: Light and new – doesn't last as long.
*   Upholstery: Like leather, fake leather, velvet, linen, or cotton.

**3. Styles**

*   Modern: Simple and clean.
*   Contemporary: Always changing with the times.
*   Traditional: Fancy wood, old-school shapes.
*   Rustic/Farmhouse: Natural wood, comfy feels.
*   Industrial: Metal showing, reused wood.
*   Bohemian: Lots of colors, different styles mixed.
*   Mid-Century Modern: Like from the 50s and 60s – simple, wood colors.
*   Scandinavian: Light wood, simple, all about being useful.

**4. How to Place Things**

*   Living Room: Be comfy + look good. Keep paths open (about 3 feet wide).
*   Dining Room: Leave 3 feet around the table.
*   Bedroom: Bed is the main thing; nightstands on each side; don't make it messy.
*   Office: Desk near windows, comfy chair.
*   Small Spaces: Use tall things, stuff that does more than one job (like a sofa bed or storage ottoman).

**💡 Lighting**

**1. Types of Light**

*   Ambient (General):

    *   The main light source (like ceiling lights, LED panels, or chandeliers).

*   Task Lighting:

    *   Light for getting things done (like reading lamps, desk lamps, or under-cabinet lights).

*   Accent Lighting:

    *   Light that brings attention to things (like picture lights, lights that wash the wall, or spotlights on art).

*   Decorative Lighting:

    *   Light that looks cool (like string lights or fancy pendants).

**2. Light Types**

*   Ceiling-mounted: Close to the ceiling, a little away from the ceiling, hanging down, fancy chandeliers.
*   Wall-mounted: Sconces, vanity lights, lights that swing out.
*   Floor Lamps: Pointing up, curved, with three legs.
*   Table Lamps: For your bedside, desk, or just to look nice.
*   Recessed Lighting: Hidden in the ceiling; looks modern.
*   Track Lighting: Lights that move on a track.

**3. Light Bulbs**

*   LED: Saves energy, lasts long, lots of colors.
*   CFL: Spiral bulbs, not used as much now.
*   Incandescent: Warm light, uses lots of energy, mostly gone now.
*   Halogen: Brighter, used for task lighting.

**4. Light Color (Kelvin)**

*   Warm White (2700K–3000K): Cozy, for homes.
*   Neutral White (3500K–4100K): Even, for kitchens and bathrooms.
*   Cool White/Daylight (5000K+): Sharp, for reading and working.

**5. Smart Lights**

*   Lights you can make brighter or dimmer.
*   Bulbs that change color (RGB).
*   Lights that turn on with motion or timers.
*   Lights you control with your phone or voice (Smart Homes).

**🎨 Décor**

**1. Wall Stuff**

*   Paintings / Art Prints / Posters
*   Things you hang on the wall / Tapestries
*   Mirrors: Make rooms brighter and bigger.
*   Wall Clocks
*   3D Panels / Wallpapers / Decals

**2. Soft Stuff**

*   Curtains &amp; Drapes: Block light, make things soft.
*   Cushions &amp; Throws: Make things cozy.
*   Rugs: Make spaces feel different; area rugs, runners, shag rugs, flat rugs.
*   Bedding: Duvets, quilts, pillow covers, bed skirts.

**3. Table &amp; Shelf Stuff**

*   Vases / Bowls / Trays
*   Books / Candles / Lanterns
*   Figurines / Sculptures
*   Indoor Plants / Fake Plants

**4. Useful Décor**

*   Room Dividers / Screens
*   Cool Baskets / Storage Boxes
*   Clocks / Thermometers / Barometers

**5. Holiday Décor**

*   Holiday themes: Lights, wreaths, table settings.
*   Holiday colors &amp; feels: Warm for fall, pastels for spring, etc.

**🛠️ How to Keep Things Nice**

*   **Furniture**
*   Dust Often: Especially wood and glass.
*   Keep it Dry: Use coasters; clean up spills right away.
*   Polish or Oil Wood: Every few months to keep it looking good.
*   Vacuum Upholstery: Every week if you use it a lot.
*   Use Pads on Furniture Legs: So you don't scratch the floor.

*   **Lighting**
*   Clean Bulbs &amp; Fixtures: Unplug and wipe them down every month.
*   Replace Bulbs That Burn Out: Right away so the light is even.
*   Check Wiring: Especially if it's old or you did it yourself.

*   **Décor**
*   Wash Soft Stuff: Follow the labels (low heat, gentle).
*   Dust Normal Stuff: Dust every week.
*   Move Décor Around: So things don't fade and the room feels fresh.

**🧭 Pro Tips**

*   Balance: big and small, heavy and light.
*   Scale &amp; Size: Furniture should fit the room.
*   Contrast: Mix materials (wood + metal, soft + hard).
*   Match: Stick to a color or style.
*   Main Thing: Have one thing that stands out – art, lighting, or furniture.
*   Layer: Use different heights and feels (like a rug + coffee table + vase).
*   Windows: Use them as much as you can with mirrors and see-through curtains.

**Quick Room-by-Room Decor Guide**

**1. Living Room**

*   **Must-Haves:**
    *   Couch (or sectional)
    *   Coffee table
    *   Side tables
    *   TV stand
    *   Bookshelves
*   **Lighting:**
    *   Main light: Ceiling light.
    *   Accent: Floor lamps or spotlights.
    *   Task: Reading lamp by your seat.
*   **Decor Ideas:**
    *   Area rug to define the seating area.
    *   Lots of pillows and blankets.
    *   Mirrors to make the room bigger.
    *   Cool art on the walls.

**2. Dining Room**

*   **Must-Haves:**
    *   Table and chairs
    *   Sideboard
    *   Maybe a bar cart
*   **Lighting:**
    *   Hanging light/Chandelier about 2.5-3 feet above the table
    *   Wall lights.
*   **Decor Ideas:**
    *   Centerpiece (vase, candles, etc.).
    *   Art or mirrors.
    *   Nice table linens.

**3. Bedroom**

*   **Must-Haves:**
    *   Bed frame
    *   Nightstands
    *   Dresser
    *   Closet
    *   Maybe a Vanity table
*   **Lighting:**
    *   Main light: Ceiling fan or flush mount.
    *   Task: Lamps.
    *   Accent: Strip lights.
*   **Decor Ideas:**
    *   Art above the bed.
    *   Soft bedding.
    *   Rug partially under the bed.
    *   Curtains.

**4. Home Office**

*   **Must-Haves:**
    *   Desk and comfy chair
    *   Shelves
    *   Filing drawers
*   **Lighting:**
    *   Task: Desk lamp.
    *   Main light: Overhead LED
    *   Natural light if you can.
*   **Decor Ideas:**
    *   Art
    *   Plants
    *   Desk organizers.

**5. Bathroom**

*   **Must-Haves:**
    *   Vanity
    *   Mirror
    *   Storage
*   **Lighting:**
    *   Vanity lights at eye level.
    *   Overhead light.
    *   Optional: LED strips.
*   **Decor Ideas:**
    *   Jars for stuff.
    *   Plants.
    *   Matching towels.

**6. Kitchen**

*   **Must-Haves:**
    *   Cabinets
    *   Island/cart
    *   Bar stools
*   **Lighting:**
    *   Main light: Ceiling lights.
    *   Task: Under-cabinet lights.
    *   Pendant lights over the island.
*   **Decor Ideas:**
    *   Open shelves.
    *   Pretty jars, cookbooks.
    *   Linens.

**7. Outdoor/Balcony**

*   **Furniture:**
    *   Chairs
    *   Hammocks
    *   Planters
    *   Storage
*   **Lighting:**
    *   String lights
    *   Lanterns
    *   Wall lights
*   **Decor:**
    *   Weather-proof fabrics.
    *   Plants.
    *   Screens for privacy.

**Cool Decor Tricks**

*   **Mix Textures:** Try something like a soft blanket on a leather couch, or a textured rug with a glass table.
*   **Color Vibes:**
    *   Blues/Greens: Chill.
    *   Reds/Oranges/Yellows: Energetic.
    *   Neutrals: Simple.
*   **Eye Movement:** Make sure your eye flows easily through the room.
*   **The Rule of 3:** Things often look better in groups of 3.
*   **Empty Space:** Don't cram everything in!

**Furniture That Does More**

*   Great for small spaces:
    *   Sofa beds
    *   Tables that get bigger
    *   Fold-down desks
    *   Storage ottomans
    *   Shelves that turn into desks

**Plants!**

*   Easy Plants: Snake plant.
*   Sunny Plants: Succulents.
*   Clean Air Plants: Peace lily.
*   Put them in corners, on shelves, hanging up...wherever!

**How Much Space Do You Need?**

*   Couch to coffee table: About a foot and a half.
*   Couch to TV: 7-9 feet.
*   Chair to wall in dining room: 3 feet.
*   Bed to wall: 2 feet.
*   Rug around table: 2-2.5 feet.
